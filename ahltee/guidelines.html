Guidelines
Don't be an idiot.
Whenever you're about to do something, ask yourself, "Would an idiot do that?" And if they would, do not do that thing.
Be concise.
When submitting text, only include enough of the passage to clearly show what has changed. We're not trying to recreate entire novels here, and going too far could venture into copyright infringement.
Only use sources you can personally verify.
You are responsible for every edit you submit, so only add information you can verify yourself with the book in front of you.
Be exact.
When creating or editing something, fill in all relevant information.
Use asterisks (*) for emphasis.
We don't have full Markdown support, but you can italicize parts of your submission by surrounding it with asterisks. Pay close attention to what punctuation is italicized. Space at the beginning or end of an italicized phrase should not be italicized, even if a digital version has it that way.
Match the style of quotation marks and apostrophes.
Standard keyboards include keys for straight quotation marks (") and apostrophes ('), but these probably don't appear in print. Use the curled versions as dictated by the text:
<<becomes“
<becomes‘
>>becomes”
>or'become’
